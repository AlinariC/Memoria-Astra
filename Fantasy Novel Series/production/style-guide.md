# Style Guide

## Prose Priorities

The books should read as full, immersive epic fantasy: clear action, textured environments, emotionally grounded dialogue, and full-length paragraphs that let the reader live in the scene.

Avoid shallow summary when the moment deserves embodiment. A battlefield should have footing, weather, sound, fear, confusion, fatigue, and tactical change. A city should have food, smell, labor, class, law, prayer, and waste. A friendship should change through choices, not just banter.

## Tone

Primary tone: cinematic, emotional, dangerous, wondrous.

Secondary texture: dry humor from pressure, not quippy modern detachment.

The world can be grand, but the prose should stay close to bodies: ash in teeth, rope-burned palms, wet boots, old scars, engine heat, bad soup, the silence after a vow.

## Naming Rules

- Prefer pronounceable names.
- Avoid decorative apostrophes unless a language rule truly needs them.
- Avoid too many similar initials in the main cast.
- Keep one spelling per person, place, faction, and magical term.
- Use invented terms only when they earn their place.

## Capitalization

Use consistent capitalization:

- cinder, cinders
- cinder-singer, cinder-singing
- the Crownreach
- the Pale Bough
- the Umbral Seat
- the Seven Arches
- Oathbound
- Vorrakai's Choir
- Book-specific titles like Cinder Saint only when used as formal titles.

## Action

Action scenes should have:

- A concrete objective.
- Spatial clarity.
- Changing conditions.
- Character-specific tactics.
- A cost.
- An emotional turn.

Large fights should move in phases without feeling like game mechanics on the page: first misunderstanding, adaptation, enemy shift, sacrifice, and a victory that comes from insight as well as force.

## Magic

Magic must follow the rules in `series_bible/01-world-and-magic.md`.

Every major magical use should cost something or change the situation permanently. The reader should feel wonder and danger at the same time.

## IP Avoidance

Do not use:

- recognizable names from existing fantasy games or novels.
- crystal-chosen warrior cosmology.
- a plot about carrying one corrupting object to destroy it in a volcanic land.
- iconic class labels in prose.
- good light elves versus evil dark elves.
- distant dark lord as vague shadow-only threat.
- hobbits or hobbit substitutes.

Use broad genre materials in original configurations with original histories, economies, and moral conflicts.
