## Core Logline

When Mara Venn, an expendable ash-runner from a poisoned mining city, discovers she can hear the buried hearts of dead dragons, she becomes the most wanted person in Vael Taryn: a tool for kings, a threat to elves, a miracle to rebels, and the only witness who can stop ancient grief from turning the living world into an obedient afterlife.

## Adaptation Hook

The Cinderwake Trilogy is a completed epic fantasy saga built around a screen-friendly engine: a powerless worker becomes the one person who can hear the buried memories that the ruling world has turned into fuel.

The story has the scale streamers want from fantasy, but the entry point is intimate and human. Mara is not a secret princess or inherited savior. She is a small, poor, practical girl who crawls through lethal mining vents because powerful people consider her replaceable. Her gift does not make her glamorous. It makes her listen.

That gives the adaptation a clear emotional question:

Can someone born powerless become powerful without becoming the kind of person who once decided her life was expendable?

## Format Recommendation

Primary recommendation: premium serialized television, 3 seasons, 8 episodes per season.

Alternate path: animated limited series or adult/family crossover animation, preserving the scale of dragons, dead cities, underground moons, vast battles, and magical environments without forcing a first-season live-action budget to carry the entire world at once.

Feature path: possible as a trilogy, but television better preserves the ensemble, faction politics, and emotional payoff of Mara's rise from local survival to world-changing witness.

## Series Shape

Season 1 / Book 1: The Ash Beneath the Crown
Mara discovers that her kingdom's warmth and military power are fed by dead dragon cinders and hidden labor. She escapes a royal cover-up, forms the first version of her company, and exposes the undead foundry beneath Harrowmere's throne.

Season 2 / Book 2: The Moon Below the World
Mara becomes a symbol before she is ready. Light elves, dark elves, rebels, and engineers all try to define her. The road leads beneath the world to a buried dragon-moon and the sealed crime that split ancient peoples into opposing courts.

Season 3 / Book 3: The Dragon That Dreamed Death
The dead rise in daylight. Dragons return as judges. The first dragon offers a world without grief, war, or choice. Mara must reject both martyrdom and rule, break the covenant that made crowns stronger than people, and free the living to govern what power remembers.

## Why It Can Travel On Screen

- A clear protagonist: Mara's physical survival skills, class position, and listening gift make the fantasy understandable without exposition-heavy worldbuilding.
- A built-in ensemble: each major companion brings a visual identity, a tactical function, a secret, and a moral argument with Mara.
- Strong seasonal escalation: mine city, haunted fen, royal foundry, underworld court, buried moon, dragon judgment, final covenant.
- A fresh dragon mythology: dragons are not just monsters, mounts, or treasure guardians. Their buried memory-organs power cities, weapons, wards, ghosts, healing, and resurrection.
- An emotionally legible villain philosophy: the final threat is not destruction for its own sake, but the seductive promise of ending suffering by ending choice.
- Completed source material: the trilogy already contains the beginning, middle, ending, character arcs, world rules, and adaptation roadmap.

## Key Characters

Mara Venn
An overlooked ash-runner from Kell Ashgate. Clever, wary, brave, and trained to expect dismissal. Her arc moves from disposable worker to leader, witness, and founder of a new covenant.

Sir Caldus Renn
A disgraced knight and tactical mentor whose broken oath becomes the story's test of whether honor can survive accountability.

Ilyr Noct-Vey
A dark-elf exile, spy, and historian whose people sealed away half of a truth because the whole truth could restart a catastrophe.

Saelith Dawnmere
A light-elf oathkeeper and healer whose reverence for law is challenged when obedience begins to harm living people.

Tavi Quen
A gnome engineer and field medic whose inventions can save cities or become weapons, depending on who controls them.

Kesh of the Kavik Road
A road-scout and negotiator who treats loyalty like a contract until friendship becomes more binding than profit.

Brakka of the Third Arch
An oath-sister and frontline force who judges vows by what they protect, not by what power says they mean.

Arveth the Named
An undead scholar and witness who reframes the dead as citizens, victims, and keepers of erased history rather than disposable monsters.

## Audience Promise

The Cinderwake Trilogy is for viewers who want large-scale fantasy with a strong emotional spine: found family, underdog leadership, factional conflict, ancient magic with a cost, dragons as moral memory, and a heroine who saves the world by refusing to spend other people the way she was spent.

## Submission Note

Do not send manuscripts, full treatments, scripts, pitch decks, or unpublished materials to studios, streamers, executives, or producers unless they have asked to receive them or their submission policy allows it. Use a short query first, then send materials only after invitation or through a representative.

Before formal entertainment-industry submission, prepare a clean rights packet:

- Confirm copyright registration status for all three novels and any cover/key art being shown.
- Confirm chain-of-title for text, images, audio, and any collaborators or production tools.
- Decide whether to pitch live-action series, animated series, or both.
- Have an entertainment attorney review any option, shopping agreement, producer attachment, or submission release.
