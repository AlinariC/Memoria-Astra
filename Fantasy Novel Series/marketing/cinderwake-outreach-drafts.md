# Cinderwake Outreach Drafts

Purpose: build permission-based paths to producers, managers, agents, attorneys, and streamer-connected production companies for The Cinderwake Trilogy.

Important: this is not a mass email plan. Major streamers and many production companies refuse unsolicited materials. The first message should ask whether they are open to a short rights/adaptation query. Send manuscripts, decks, or longer treatments only after invitation or through a representative.

## Best Route

1. Entertainment attorney, manager, or literary/film rights agent.
2. Producer or production company with fantasy, YA/adult crossover, animation, or book adaptation experience and a stated query/submission path.
3. Warm intro to a producer, development executive, or agency contact.
4. Only after representation or producer attachment: streamer pitch routes such as Netflix or Amazon MGM/Prime Video.

## Streamer Reality Check

Netflix's official help center says unsolicited ideas/materials are not accepted. Netflix says projects must come through a licensed agent, producer, attorney, manager, or industry executive with an existing Netflix relationship.

Amazon's public Prime Video Slate documentation is for finished content partners, delivery, and aggregation services, not for submitting a book or raw adaptation pitch directly to Amazon MGM Studios.

Practical implication: do not cold-send The Cinderwake Trilogy directly to Netflix or Amazon executives. Build the package, get a rep or producer attachment, and let that person submit through a proper relationship.

## What Not To Send First

- Do not attach full manuscripts in the first message.
- Do not paste long synopses into cold emails.
- Do not claim Netflix, Amazon, or any named studio is already interested.
- Do not imply the project is submitted to a streamer unless a proper representative or invited producer has actually submitted it.
- Do not sign an option, shopping agreement, or submission release without attorney review.

## Minimum Materials Before Sending

- One-page pitch: ready.
- Short query email: ready.
- Proof of completed source material: available locally.
- Series bible: available locally.
- Rights/chain-of-title checklist: still needs legal review before serious negotiation.
- Contact list: still needs approved, current recipients.
