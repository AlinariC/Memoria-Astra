# Book Two Outline: The Moon Below the World

Target length: 140,000 to 170,000 words.

Estimated finished pages: 460 to 560.

Primary promise: Mara becomes a symbol, loses control of what others do in her name, and descends into Orrowen to stop a war between light and dark elves.

Complete book arc: the elven holy war is stopped, Serathiel is defeated, the dead of Orrowen are freed from immediate enslavement, and the party survives a major fracture.

Series hook: the dragon-moon beneath the world opens one eye.

## Part I: The Burden of Banners

Mara has saved Kell Ashgate, but now rebel towns raise her mark without permission. Human rulers call her a terrorist. The Pale Bough calls her a Cinder Saint. Khar Veyl calls her an extinction risk. Goblin caravans sell songs about her before she has decided what the songs mean.

Key movement:

- Mara tries to help refugees and discovers leadership creates consequences faster than good intentions can manage.
- Saelith's order sends an escort that is half honor guard, half cage.
- Ilyr receives a summons from Khar Veyl: bring Mara below or be erased from memory-law.
- Tavi identifies a continent-wide cinder pattern that should be impossible unless something under Orrowen is waking.

## Part II: Lumenorath

The party enters the light-elf realm. Lumenorath is astonishing: white-root towers, mirror-bark halls, coral gardens, law sung at dawn, grief folded into art. It is also a society where dissent disappears inside ceremonies of preservation.

Set pieces:

- A moonlit raid through a floating orchard whose fruit stores stolen spells.
- Saelith's trial before the Pale Bough.
- A public hymn that nearly overwrites Mara's own memories with a saintly version of herself.
- Serathiel's private mercy, which makes her more frightening rather than less.

Part turn: Serathiel declares a cleansing campaign against Khar Veyl, claiming the dark elves hid the original dragon crime.

## Part III: Khar Veyl

The descent to the dark-elf under-realms is not a fall into evil. It is a passage into a people who survived by making truth difficult to steal. The halls are black basalt, root-fire, blade schools, exile courts, and banquet politics.

Set pieces:

- The Banquet of True Shadows, where every lie changes the room's light.
- Ilyr's hidden crime: he exposed a false story but concealed evidence that his own house profited from the lie.
- Kesh bargains with dark-elf smugglers and loses something more binding than money.
- Mara learns the ancient light/dark split was engineered to hide the burial site of a dragon-moon.

Part turn: Khar Veyl's hardliners plan to strike Lumenorath first. Both sides are willing to begin the war they claim to fear.

## Part IV: Blackroot Road

The party descends through fungal forests, bone-lit waterways, gnome engine ruins, and battlefields where the dead still pray. Orrowen is introduced as a civilization that remembers itself too strongly to end.

Set pieces:

- A retreat across salt flats while undead cavalry rise first in reflections.
- A gnome engine-temple flooding with liquid glass.
- Arveth confronting a courthouse where his death sentence is still being reissued every morning.
- Mara hearing Vorrakai clearly for the first time and mistaking grief for wisdom.

Part turn: Serathiel and the dark-elf hardliners both reach Orrowen. The war moves underground.

## Part V: The Moon Below the World

The climax happens around the sealed dragon-moon, a vast cinder-body used by the ancient elves to bind a piece of Vorrakai's dream. Serathiel wants to cleanse it. Khar Veyl wants to keep it imprisoned at any cost. The Choir wants both sides to break it.

Key beats:

- Saelith renounces the Pale Bough's command publicly.
- Ilyr reveals the full historical record, incriminating both light and dark courts.
- Tavi chooses not to use a device that would win the battle by enslaving the dead.
- Brakka forces both armies into oath-law by declaring the chamber a bridge between histories.
- Mara names the dead of Orrowen as witnesses, preventing either army from claiming purity.
- Serathiel falls, not because she is weak, but because her perfect order cannot survive honest memory.

Resolution:

The holy war is stopped. The party's alliance becomes real but fragile. Mara has prevented the dead from becoming weapons, but she has also opened the seal enough for the dragon-moon to wake.

Ending image:

Deep under the world, a horizon curves where no horizon should be. An eye opens in the moon below, and every cinder in Vael Taryn answers.
