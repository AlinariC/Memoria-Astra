# Book One Outline: The Ash Beneath the Crown

Target length: 125,000 to 150,000 words.

Estimated finished pages: 400 to 500.

Primary promise: Mara Venn rises from expendable ash-runner to leader of a city rebellion, forms the core fellowship, and destroys the royal undead foundry beneath Harrowmere's crown.

Complete book arc: Kell Ashgate is saved, Lord Cael Mordane is defeated, the first foundry is destroyed, and Mara earns her own voice.

Series hook: the shattered cinder beneath the foundry speaks with many dragon voices: "You woke us too soon."

## Part I: The Girl in the Vent

Purpose: establish Mara's world, the exploitation of Kell Ashgate, the first hint of cinder-singing, and the disappearance that forces action.

Key chapters:

1. **The Girl in the Vent** - Mara crawls a lethal cinder vent for extra pay and hears something under the stone say her name.
2. **Ash Wages** - the miners are short-paid after a royal inspection; Joryn warns Mara to stay useful and silent.
3. **The Bell That Rang Back** - a floodbell rings with no hand on the rope, and Mara alone hears a second bell underground.
4. **The Minister's Mercy** - Lord Cael Mordane arrives promising winter relief in exchange for more workers.
5. **The Unnamed Dead** - Mara finds a corpse moving in the slag pits with a royal work tag sewn into its throat.
6. **A Knight Without a Crest** - Caldus Renn saves Mara from arrest, then tries to hand her over until the dead recognize him.
7. **The First Spark** - Mara answers the cinder by accident and burns a command sigil out of an undead soldier's skull.

Part turn: Mara learns that the crown is taking miners who "die" in accidents and sending their bodies to a hidden foundry.

## Part II: Roads Nobody Owns

Purpose: force Mara out of Kell Ashgate, assemble the first allies, and make the world feel larger.

Key chapters:

8. **Kesh's Bad Bargain** - Kesh sells Mara a route through the Gloamfen, then admits someone paid him more to delay her.
9. **The Market Under the Sleeping Beast** - goblin salvage culture, black humor, and a monster ecology set piece.
10. **Memory Moths** - Mara's guilt attracts a swarm that reveals she heard the cinder before and forgot.
11. **Brakka's Toll** - the party reaches the Seven Arches and must swear a crossing-vow.
12. **Siege of Third Arch** - royal troops attack while trolls debate whether the bridge is road, shrine, or ancestor.
13. **A Vow Is a Road** - Mara keeps a promise no one expected her to keep, winning Brakka as an ally.
14. **The Dead Remember Names** - Arveth appears, proving at least some undead retain personhood.

Part turn: the foundry is larger than a city secret. It is connected to Harrowmere's coronation.

## Part III: The Crown's Shadow

Purpose: infiltrate the political world, complicate good and evil, and introduce elf and gnome power centers.

Key chapters:

15. **Tavi's Impossible Lock** - Tavi Quen opens a royal cinder gate and nearly kills everyone with her own brilliance.
16. **The Pale Envoy** - Saelith Dawnmere offers protection that sounds too much like ownership.
17. **The Exile at the Window** - Ilyr Noct-Vey tries to kill Mara, then stops when her gift hears the truth he is hiding.
18. **Saint-General's Letter** - the Pale Bough wants Mara delivered to Lumenorath.
19. **The Ash-Marked Chapel** - Caldus confesses he once guarded a prisoner transfer to the foundry.
20. **The King's Winter Speech** - Harrowmere celebrates stability while hidden furnaces warm the palace.
21. **Drowned Archive Raid** - the party steals proof from an archive where healing magic attracts predators.
22. **The Price of Evidence** - proof is useless unless someone with power agrees the victims count.

Part turn: Mara stops trying to find a noble rescuer and decides Kell Ashgate must rescue itself.

## Part IV: Foundry of the Unnamed

Purpose: rebellion planning, relationship pressure, and the first major dungeon.

Key chapters:

23. **Ashgate Rises Quietly** - miners, goblins, outcast soldiers, and troll masons prepare a rebellion disguised as routine work.
24. **The Engine Below** - the foundry is a dungeon of cinder furnaces, corpse-rails, oath hooks, and memory locks.
25. **The Tactical Mirror** - the party must defeat a reflection of its own formation.
26. **Joryn's Chain** - Mara finds her brother among living prisoners tagged for conversion.
27. **Mordane's Arithmetic** - Cael Mordane argues that a kingdom has the right to spend invisible lives.
28. **The Dead Refuse** - Arveth teaches Mara to name the dead, breaking one production line.
29. **Cinderbreak** - Tavi's device can overload the foundry but will wake the buried dragon-heart.

Part turn: the rebellion succeeds too early. The foundry begins waking under the palace.

## Part V: Ash Beneath the Crown

Purpose: climax, complete victory, cost, and trilogy escalation.

Key chapters:

30. **Coronation of the Dead** - Mordane unleashes royal undead during the coronation to secure permanent rule.
31. **The Bridge Comes to the City** - Brakka and the trolls turn city architecture into a battlefield.
32. **No Saint, No Weapon** - Saelith tries to sanctify Mara publicly; Mara refuses to be made symbol before person.
33. **Names in the Furnace** - Mara speaks names through the cinder and the enslaved dead begin choosing.
34. **Caldus Keeps the Wrong Oath** - Caldus breaks his knightly oath to keep a human one.
35. **Mordane's Last Number** - Mordane is defeated by the workers he never counted.
36. **You Woke Us Too Soon** - the cinder shatters, the city survives, and dragon voices answer from across Vael Taryn.
37. **The Road After Victory** - Kell Ashgate is free but exposed; Mara chooses to leave before armies arrive.

Ending mood: earned triumph with wider dread. The reader should feel satisfied that Book One's villains and local injustice were confronted, while urgently wanting to know what the waking cinders mean.
